The person behind Wanwun

Namaskar — I'm Sahil.

I'm a Kashmiri, a builder, and someone who grew up a little far from the traditions that have defined me and my identity.

Somewhere between chasing printed books for every festival and explaining our calendar to the next generation, I realised our community needed one warm, modern place to call home. So I started building Wanwun.

Why I built this

Our community is scattered across the world, and so is everything that keeps us connected — the panchang, the festival dates, the bhajans, the stories our elders carry. I was tired of it living in a dozen half-remembered places. Wanwun gathers it into one app that feels like ours: computed for your city, in our language, made with care.

Where it's going

Panchang and the Bhajan library are just the first pillar. The vision is a true community home — the Baithak for conversation, shared events and gatherings, a place to preserve our recipes, rituals and history, and the wisdom of our elders. I want Wanwun to grow into a gathering place the whole diaspora can reach for.

I'm always listening

This isn't my platform — it's ours, and it's better every time someone tells me what they'd love to see. A feature, a festival I've missed, a bhajan, a bug, or just a hello: I read every message myself. If you care about our traditions, I'd genuinely love to build this with you.

This is just the beginning.

Wanwun is being built in the open, one pillar at a time, for a community I love. If you have an idea, want to help, or simply want to say hello — my inbox is always open.
